Part 1: Publishing Data

Provider's perspective

Step 1 - I log in on my Connector

Provider: Will

Will opens his participant portal:
https://caney-fork.glcdi.startinblox.com/catalogue/

He clicks "Log in" and authenticates via Keycloak.

Behind the scenes: Will's local Keycloak brokers to the governance Keycloak, which adds GLCDI claims to his token:

  • glcdi_membership: "active"
  • glcdi_roles: ["glcdi_member", "glcdi_producer"]
  • glcdi_certification_status: "regenerative-verified"
  • glcdi_contribution_status: "contributing"

Will doesn't see any of this — he just logs in.

Step 2 - I go to "My Assets"

Provider: Will

Will navigates to the "My Assets" section.

He sees his previously published datasets:

  • Grazing Rotation Schedule
  • Paddock Boundaries (GeoJSON)
  • NDVI Vegetation Index

He clicks "Publish a new dataset".

Step 4 — I associate policies

Provider: Will

Before the dataset appears in other participants' catalogs,
Will must attach policies that define the rules of engagement.

The connector presents pre-defined policy templates
created by the GLCDI governance team.

Will must choose two types of policies…

Two types of policies

Step 5 — Will selects his policies

Provider: Will

For his SOC measurements, Will chooses:

Access: Researchers only
Only Point Blue, University of Florida, and similar research participants can see this dataset.

Contract (combined):

  • Model training only — restricted purpose
  • Time-limited — expires September 2026
  • Attribution required — cite Caney Fork
  • Anonymisation required — remove farm name & GPS
  • Share back insights — return model predictions
"Researchers can see this dataset. They can use it for model training until September 2026. They must anonymise it, cite me, and share their results back."

Step 6 — I publish my dataset

Provider: Will

Will clicks "Publish".

Behind the scenes, the connector creates three objects:

  1. Asset — the dataset description + data source
  2. Policy Definitions — the selected access + contract policies
  3. Contract Definition — the binding: asset + policies

The dataset is now live. It will appear in the catalog of any participant who satisfies the access policy.

Will sees: "SOC Measurements published. Visible to: researchers and data stewards."

The data itself never moves — it stays on Will's infrastructure.
The connector is the gatekeeper.

Step 10 — Data transfer

Consumer: Elena

With an active contract, Elena clicks "Download Dataset".

Her connector sends a Transfer Request to Caney Fork's connector.

Caney Fork's connector:

  1. Verifies a valid contract agreement exists
  2. Serves the SOC data via the HTTP data plane

Elena receives the dataset in her connector's data store.

The transfer is logged on both sides — an auditable record of who accessed what, when, and under which contract terms.

Reciprocity: Elena shares back

Consumer: Elena Provider: Will

Six months later, Elena has trained her SOC prediction model.

She publishes her model outputs as a new dataset
on Point Blue's connector, targeting Caney Fork.

Will receives a notification and sees Elena's predictions:

"Based on your current rotation schedule, SOC at 0–10cm depth is projected to increase by 0.3% annually over the next 5 years."

The reciprocity loop is complete:

  • Will shared raw SOC data
  • Elena trained a model
  • Elena shared predictions back with Will
  • Will gets actionable insights for his farm

Data providers get value back. That's the core promise of the GLCDI data space.

What happens when the contract expires?

System

September 30, 2026 — the prototype phase ends.

  • Elena tries to negotiate new access to SOC data
  • Caney Fork's connector automatically rejects: time constraint expired
  • Existing data on Elena's system is still there (no remote deletion)
  • Retention obligations from the DSA still apply

To continue sharing, Will must:

  1. Review the partnership
  2. Publish a new policy with an updated expiry
  3. Elena negotiates a new contract

This creates a natural consent renewal point — Will decides each phase whether to continue sharing, with whom, and under what terms.

Key principle: The provider defines the rules once. The connector enforces them automatically for every consumer. No manual approval needed per request — trust is encoded in the policies.

What if the consumer doesn't match?

Access policy fails

A corporate analyst queries Caney Fork's catalog.

SOC Measurements has researchers-only access.

The analyst's role is glcdi_corporate.

The dataset is invisible.
The analyst doesn't know it exists.

Contract policy fails

A researcher sees the dataset (access passes).

They declare purpose: Scope3Reporting.

The policy only allows AgronomicModelTraining.

Negotiation is rejected.
They must use a different dataset
or contact the provider.
